Creating the Scatterplot 2

A scatterplot is developed using two or more measures. Start by downloading the excel file that will be used, Colaberry Amazing Supermart. To the right, under Connect> To a File>Microsoft Excel. Navigate to the saved excel file.

 

After selecting the Colaberry Amazing Supermart excel file, it will appear in the Connections. Below connections, the sheets of the file are displayed. (This file has only one sheet)

Click at the bottom of the screen to add a new worksheet.

Once you have a new worksheet open, be sure to provide a meaningful name. Right click sheet1 to highlight and rename this worksheet Sales/Profits by Customer. 

Notice that the column headings from excel have been separated into Dimensions and Measures. Start by selecting  Sales from measures and dragging this to the Columns area and selecting Profits from measures and dragging this to the Rows area. This will produce a single point on your sheet which represents the sum of all profits and sum of all sales.   


SUM of Profit (X-axis) and the SUM of Sales (y-axis).

 In order to add additional granularity to the scatterplot, click and drag the Customer Name dimension (pill) to the shelf and drop it on top of Details.

Drag and drop Customer name on top of labels. This will add a mark to the chart that represents each customer, as well as add a label with the customer’s name. 

To change the appearance of the mark, select shape and choose desired visual. For this instance, a filled in circle is selected. Additionally, drag Profit from Measures to size. This will change the appearance making the more profitable customers have larger marks and the less profitable customers have smaller marks. By selecting color, the opacity of the marks can be diminished to aid in visibility.



To learn more about building a scatterplot in Tableau, click on the link! https://onlinehelp.tableau.com/current/pro/desktop/en-us/buildexamples_scatter.htm
